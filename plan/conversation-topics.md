# Conversation Topics — Reference

Standing reference list of everyday conversation topics for living, working, and socializing
in Japan. Used to plan voice conversation practice and vocabulary/phrase prep. Not tied to a
specific week — revisit and reuse across weeks.

For each topic below, fill in:
- **My notes (Claude)** — what I think the topic should cover
- **Your notes** — what you think should also be covered

---

## 1. Weather (天気)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Small-talk opener/closer — the reflexive "いい天気ですね" ("nice weather, isn't it?") used to
  start or end almost any conversation, even with strangers
- Describing current conditions — hot/cold/humid/chilly, sunny/cloudy/rainy, wind, describing
  how it feels (蒸し暑い = muggy)
- Forecast talk — checking/sharing tomorrow's forecast, chance of rain, deciding whether to
  bring an umbrella
- Seasonal transitions — commenting on the change of seasons (temperature swings, cherry
  blossoms starting, first snow, rainy season/梅雨 starting or ending)
- Typhoons — a very common real topic in Japan: typhoon warnings, train delays/service
  suspensions, safety precautions, comparing this year's typhoon season
- Extreme weather small talk — heatwave warnings (熱中症/heatstroke concern), heavy snow
  disrupting trains, advice like "stay hydrated" or "be careful going home"
- Weather affecting plans — "let's cancel/reschedule because of the rain," "let's go if it
  clears up"
- Comparing weather — comparing today vs. yesterday, comparing regions (Tokyo vs. Hokkaido),
  comparing to home country

**Your notes:**
- Earthquakes and tsunamis — what to do during these events, prevention/preparedness,
  evacuation zones (避難場所)
- Mobile phone emergency alerts (緊急地震速報 / J-Alert) — understanding the actual Japanese
  wording used in these alerts
- Rain gear vocabulary — umbrella, rain boots, rain cover, raincoat, and related items
- Comparing weather in Japan vs. Europe (e.g. London, Hungary)
- Full comprehension of TV/radio weather forecasts — understanding all vocabulary and phrasing
  used in an actual broadcast forecast, not just simplified versions

## 2. Work / Company (仕事・会社)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Basic job description — what you do, your job title, your company, industry, department
- Daily work small talk — commute (満員電車/rush hour trains), what time you start/finish,
  overtime (残業), being busy vs. having free time
- Meetings & tasks — scheduling a meeting, giving a status update, asking for/giving deadlines,
  asking for help, reporting a problem
- Hierarchy & politeness register — talking to boss (上司) vs. colleagues (同僚) vs.
  subordinates — keigo (敬語)/humble-polite forms, since this is one of the few topics where
  casual speech isn't appropriate
- Business trips & remote work — 出張 (business trip), テレワーク/在宅勤務 (remote work), video
  calls
- Complaints/venting (mild) — being tired, stressful days, difficult coworkers/clients —
  common izakaya-style small talk
- Career/company topics — changing jobs (転職), promotions, new hires, retirement, company
  events (忘年会/歓迎会)
- Sick days / time off — calling in sick, requesting vacation (有給), returning after leave
- Introducing yourself at work — first-day self-introduction (自己紹介), exchanging business
  cards (名刺交換) — a very codified ritual in Japan
- Talking about foreign work experience — comparing work culture in Japan vs. your home country

**Your notes:**
- Working from home — benefits and disadvantages, discussing trade-offs
- AI at work (high level) — how AI is used, what benefits people see, what struggles/challenges
  come up
- Finding out others' work pain points — asking about and understanding others' challenges at
  work, learning the vocabulary used to describe those challenges
- Changing jobs / job search — job hunting (転職活動), interview preparation, preparing a CV/resume
- Recruiter calls — understanding and answering questions from a recruiter in Japanese (e.g.
  whether you're looking for work) and explaining what you're looking for next

## 3. Food (食べ物・グルメ)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Talking about a meal — what you ate, how it tasted (美味しい/まずい), texture/spice level,
  portion size
- At a restaurant — reading a menu, ordering, asking for recommendations (おすすめ), asking
  about ingredients/allergies, asking for the bill
- Cooking at home — recipes, ingredients, cooking methods (焼く/煮る/揚げる etc.), asking
  for/giving a recipe
- Food preferences & dietary needs — likes/dislikes, allergies, vegetarian/vegan, spice
  tolerance
- Regional & seasonal specialties — famous regional dishes (ご当地グルメ), seasonal foods,
  souvenirs (お土産) that are food-related
- Grocery shopping — supermarket vocabulary, asking where something is, expiration dates,
  fresh vs. frozen
- Drinking culture — izakaya ordering, nomikai (飲み会) etiquette, non-alcoholic options
- Eating out with others — splitting the bill (割り勘), inviting someone to eat, complimenting
  the host's cooking
- Health/diet talk — dieting, cutting sugar/carbs, eating healthy vs. indulging
- Comparing cuisines — comparing Japanese food to your home country's food, explaining a dish
  from your country to a Japanese person

**Your notes:**
- Drinks in depth — alcohol types: wine (and wine varieties), shochu, sake, beer, craft beer;
  how they're made; comparing them to European drinks
- Booking a restaurant — making/asking about a reservation
- Leading conversation with restaurant staff — asking for advice/recommendations, complaining
  about food, asking for something missing that wasn't brought, asking when the meal will be
  ready, asking how a dish is made, complimenting the food/chef
- Counter-seating conversation — small talk specifically with the chef when seated at the
  counter (a common restaurant layout in Japan)
- Table manners — general Japanese dining etiquette
- Food-specific etiquette — e.g. ramen shop culture (eat quickly, don't linger, so turnover
  stays high for the shop) and other dish-specific customs

## 4. Family (家族)

Note: overlaps with #11 (Everyday Life at Home) — this topic is aimed more at *talking about*
your family to others, while #11 covers in-the-moment household phrases.

**My notes (Claude):**
- Introducing your family — describing spouse, kids, parents, siblings, ages, occupations
- Talking about your son — age, personality, what nursery/school he attends, milestones
  (walking, talking, starting school)
- Parenting talk with other parents/teachers — comparing kids' development, discipline
  approaches, sleep/eating habits, discussing with nursery staff (先生) how your child is doing
- Extended family & relationships — grandparents, visiting family, family gatherings, family
  living abroad
- Marriage & relationship talk — how you met your spouse, wedding, anniversaries
- Family events & milestones — birthdays, Shichi-Go-San (七五三), entrance ceremonies (入学式),
  other child-related Japanese rituals
- Health & wellbeing of family members — asking how someone's family is doing, illness,
  recovery
- Family plans — vacations, moving, schooling decisions, long-term plans (e.g. staying in
  Japan long-term)
- Elder care — parents aging, remote caregiving, visiting parents in your home country
- Cultural comparison — comparing family life/expectations in Japan vs. your home country
  (e.g. Hungary)

**Your notes:**
- Asking for / giving parenting advice — asking others' advice on specific parenting
  situations, and giving advice to others on specific parenting topics
- Nursery teacher communication — asking the nursery teacher (先生) questions about your son's
  day, and giving updates to the teacher about him (e.g. "he hit his head yesterday, so please
  be careful")

## 5. Hobbies (趣味)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Introducing your hobbies — what you do in your free time, how often, since when
- Asking about others' hobbies — "what do you do for fun," following up with interested
  questions
- Sports — playing vs. watching, favorite teams, going to games, talking about a match
- Media & entertainment — anime/manga, movies, music, games, reading — recommending and asking
  for recommendations
- Travel as a hobby — trips taken/planned, favorite destinations (overlaps lightly with #10)
- Outdoor activities — hiking, camping, cycling, walking in the park with family
- Creative/skill hobbies — photography, cooking as a hobby, crafts, learning an instrument
- Explaining a hobby in detail — describing rules of a game/sport, equipment needed, where you
  do it
- Talking about skill level & progress — beginner vs. experienced, how long you've practiced,
  wanting to improve
- Making plans around hobbies — inviting someone to join an activity, discussing
  schedules/clubs/circles (サークル)

**Your notes:**
- Personal hobby set — food and cooking (may overlap with #3), traveling (may overlap with
  #10), interest in understanding new technology
- Past hobbies you no longer actively do — jogging and Spartan Race (used to do it, don't do
  it much these days), dancing (used to dance, doesn't dance much these days) — includes the
  grammar/phrasing for "I used to do X" vs. "these days I don't do X much"

## 6. Health (健康)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Describing symptoms — headache, fever, cold, stomachache, pain location, how long it's lasted
- At the doctor/pharmacy — describing symptoms to a doctor, understanding instructions, asking
  about medicine dosage, over-the-counter vs. prescription
- Health checkups — 健康診断 (annual health checkup, common at Japanese workplaces), blood
  tests, results
- Exercise & fitness — talking about workouts, gym routines, running, staying in shape
- Sleep — sleep quality, insomnia, being tired, sleep schedule
- Diet & nutrition — eating healthy, cutting down on something, managing weight
- Mental health & stress — talking about stress, feeling overwhelmed, work-life balance,
  relaxation
- Minor injuries — cuts, bruises, sprains — especially relevant given your son's nursery
  bump-on-the-head example
- Insurance & procedures — health insurance card (保険証), hospital vs. clinic (病院 vs.
  クリニック), making an appointment
- Wishing someone well — "get well soon" (お大事に), asking how someone is recovering, checking
  in on a sick coworker/family member

**Your notes:**
- Pharmacy vocabulary — names of specific medicines and items (thermometer, plaster/bandage,
  and other common pharmacy items), so you can ask for them by name
- Japanese diet culture — e.g. bento box culture (a little of everything in one meal),
  comparing Japan's approach to diet/healthy eating with other countries, so you can compliment
  Japanese colleagues on it or discuss it with non-Japanese colleagues

## 7. Housing / Neighborhood (住まい・近所)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Describing where you live — apartment vs. house, size (LDK notation), floor, area/
  neighborhood name
- Apartment hunting — talking to a real estate agent (不動産屋), viewing a property, key terms
  (敷金/礼金 deposit/key money, 家賃 rent)
- Moving — moving in/out, utilities setup, notifying your ward office (区役所) of address change
- Neighbors — greeting neighbors, noise considerations, neighborhood associations (町内会),
  trash rules (very neighborhood-specific in Japan)
- Around the neighborhood — recommending/asking about local shops, restaurants, parks, clinics,
  supermarkets
- Home maintenance & repairs — reporting a problem to the landlord/管理会社 (management
  company), calling a repair person
- Local government/admin — city hall procedures, resident registration, local notices/回覧板
  (neighborhood circular)
- Safety & convenience — talking about how safe/convenient the area is, proximity to station,
  commute time
- Comparing neighborhoods — comparing your area to other parts of Tokyo/Japan, or to where you
  used to live
- Renovation/interior talk — furnishing a home, renovation plans, buying furniture/appliances

**Your notes:**
- Inviting a neighbor out — asking when a neighbor is free to go to a restaurant, grab drinks,
  or meet up after work or during the week

## 8. Seasonal Events (季節の行事)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Cherry blossom season (花見) — hanami parties, viewing spots, timing/forecast of bloom
- Summer festivals — 祭り, fireworks (花火大会), yukata, festival food stalls
- Obon (お盆) — visiting family graves, summer holidays, returning to hometown (帰省)
- Autumn — 紅葉狩り (leaf viewing), harvest foods, autumn comfort talk
- New Year's (お正月) — countdown, first shrine visit (初詣), New Year's food (おせち),
  greetings (あけましておめでとう)
- Christmas & Valentine's (Japan-style) — how these are celebrated differently than in the
  West (e.g. Christmas as a couples' event, illumination lights)
- Golden Week & other holiday clusters — travel plans, crowds, what people typically do
- School/work year milestones — graduation (卒業式), entrance ceremony (入学式), new fiscal year
  in April, job-hunting season
- Comparing seasonal customs — how holidays/seasons are celebrated in Japan vs. your home
  country
- Gift-giving customs — お中元/お歳暮 (mid-year and year-end gifts), White Day, souvenir-giving
  culture around trips

**Your notes:**
- Simple explanations of each event — for every seasonal event above, be able to explain in
  Japanese, in a simple way, what it is and what it's about
- Event-specific set phrases and customs — for each event, learn the special things people say
  to each other (fixed greetings/expressions) and the special things people do (rituals,
  customs) during that event

## 9. News / Current Events (ニュース・時事)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Reacting to news — "did you see/hear about...", expressing surprise/concern/interest in a
  neutral way
- Economy topics — prices going up (物価上昇), yen exchange rate, cost of living discussions —
  very common casual topic right now
- Sports news — big matches/tournaments, national team results, Olympics
- Local/community news — something happening in your area, a new store opening, construction,
  local incidents
- Weather-related news — typhoon/earthquake news updates (ties into #1's disaster-alert
  vocabulary)
- Technology news — new gadgets, AI developments (ties into your #2 AI-at-work interest)
- Entertainment news — celebrity news, new movie/drama releases, trending topics
- Safe framing for sensitive topics — how to acknowledge a political/economic topic exists
  without diving into opinions, deflecting gracefully
- Asking someone's opinion neutrally — "what do you think about...", giving a mild/hedged
  opinion yourself (Japanese conversational hedging is important here)
- International news as it relates to you — news from your home country (Hungary), being asked
  about it by Japanese people

**Your notes:**
- Asking others about their news habits — what news they follow, what sources they use, and
  what news topics they're most interested in

## 10. Travel (旅行)

**My notes (Claude):**
- Talking about past trips — where you went, with whom, how long, highlights
- Planning a trip — discussing destinations, dates, budget, deciding together
- Booking logistics — flights, hotels/ryokan, trains (shinkansen reservations), rental cars
- At the airport/station — check-in, security, boarding, asking for directions, luggage
- Sightseeing talk — recommending/asking about tourist spots, temples/shrines, museums, taking
  photos
- Domestic travel in Japan — onsen etiquette, ryokan customs, regional trains/buses, local
  specialties while traveling
- Travel problems — delays, lost luggage, missing a train, asking for help when lost
- Souvenirs & gift-giving from trips — お土産 culture — buying and giving souvenirs to
  coworkers/neighbors, a very codified Japanese custom
- Comparing travel experiences — comparing this trip to a previous one, comparing travel
  style/customs in Japan vs. abroad
- Family travel specifics — traveling with a young child — logistics, what to pack,
  kid-friendly spots (ties into #4/#11)

**Your notes:**
- Asking others about their travels — asking where they went, and asking for their
  recommendations

## 11. Everyday Life at Home / Family (家での日常会話)

Casual, in-the-moment phrases used at home — e.g. "let's make breakfast," "let's clean up the
dishes," "let's tidy up," "let's go to the park."

**My notes (Claude):**
- Morning routine — waking each other up, "did you sleep well?", getting dressed, "let's make
  breakfast," "what do you want to eat?"
- Meals — inviting to eat (itadakimasu), commenting on taste, asking for seconds, "I'm full,"
  clearing the table, washing/drying dishes, putting food away
- Household chores — cleaning up, laundry (washing, folding, hanging), taking out the trash,
  vacuuming, whose turn it is, dividing chores
- Going out together — "let's go to the park," "get ready," checking if doors are locked,
  deciding who brings what, errands/shopping
- Coming and going — "I'm leaving" / "I'm home" exchanges, asking what time someone will be
  back, welcoming someone back
- Requests & small commands — "can you pass me...", "turn off the light," "wait a second,"
  "hurry up," "be careful"
- Checking in / small talk — "how was your day," "are you tired," "what's wrong," casual
  catching up in the evening
- Evening/bedtime routine — bath time, brushing teeth, tidying before bed, "goodnight,"
  turning off lights
- Weekend/day-off planning — deciding what to do together, splitting chores vs. leisure time
- Politeness register note — mostly plain form (だ/casual verbs) rather than polite です/ます,
  plus casual imperatives and ~しよう-style invitations ("let's...")

**Your notes:**
- Talking with son about nursery/daycare — asking what he did today
- Discipline moments — when he misbehaves or makes a mistake, asking "why did you do that,"
  explaining why to be good and why not to do bad things
- Reading stories to him — physical picture books, stories on Spotify, and showing old picture
  pages/illustrations while reading
- Explaining how things work — starting from basic explanations appropriate for a young child
- Household admin / bills — paying water, electricity, gas, rent, credit card bills, etc.
