#### What it is & what to say/do
花見は春に桜の花を見ながら、公園などで家族や友達、同僚と食べたり飲んだりする行事です。桜は数日から一週間ほどで散ってしまうので、満開のタイミングを見計らって行きます。
(Hanami is an event in spring where people view cherry blossoms while eating and drinking with family, friends, or coworkers in a park. Since the blossoms fall within about a week, people time their visit for full bloom.)

- 桜がきれいですね — a standard opener under the trees, "The cherry blossoms are beautiful, aren't they"
- 満開ですね — "They're in full bloom, aren't they"
- お花見に行きませんか — "Would you like to go cherry blossom viewing?"
- 場所取りしておくね — "I'll go reserve a spot (with the sheet)"
- Custom: arriving early (sometimes hours ahead) to lay out a blue leisure sheet (レジャーシート) and claim a spot under the trees
- Custom: company hanami parties where a junior employee is sent ahead to hold the spot
- Custom: eating dango (花見団子) and drinking sake/beer under the trees
- Custom: viewing cherry blossoms illuminated at night (夜桜) and taking photos

#### Sentences & Phrases
- 今年の桜はいつ満開になりますか — When will the cherry blossoms be in full bloom this year?
- 開花予想を見ましたか — Did you see the bloom forecast?
- 桜前線が北上しています — The cherry blossom front is moving north.
- 来週末が見頃だそうです — I heard next weekend is the best time to see them.
- 公園でお花見をしましょう — Let's do hanami in the park.
- 場所取りは何時からできますか — From what time can we reserve a spot?
- 桜がもう散り始めています — The cherry blossoms have already started to fall.
- 花びらが風で舞っています — The petals are dancing in the wind.
- 夜桜も綺麗ですよ — The cherry blossoms at night are beautiful too.
- お弁当とビールを持って行きましょう — Let's bring a bento and some beer.
- 今年は花粉症がひどいです — My hay fever is bad this year.
- 桜の下で写真を撮りましょう — Let's take photos under the cherry blossoms.

#### Conversation
A: 週末、みんなでお花見に行かない？ — Do you want to go cherry blossom viewing with everyone this weekend?
B: いいね！もう満開なの？ — Sounds good! Is it already in full bloom?
A: うん、開花予想だと今週末が見頃だって — Yeah, according to the bloom forecast, this weekend is the best time.
B: じゃあ、場所取りしないとね — Then we need to reserve a spot.
A: 朝早く行って、レジャーシートを敷いておくよ — I'll go early in the morning and lay out the picnic sheet.
B: ありがとう。お弁当は私が作るね — Thanks. I'll make the bento.
A: 夜まで残って夜桜も見ようか — Should we stay until night and see the cherry blossoms lit up too?
B: いいね、寒くならないように上着持って行く — Sounds good, I'll bring a jacket so it doesn't get too cold.

#### What it is & what to say/do
夏祭りは夏の夜に神社や公園などで行われるお祭りで、屋台で食べ物を買ったり、盆踊りを踊ったり、花火大会を見たりします。浴衣を着て友達や恋人と一緒に行くのが定番です。
(Summer festivals are held at shrines or parks on summer nights; people buy food from stalls, dance the Bon dance, and watch fireworks displays. It's typical to wear a yukata and go with friends or a partner.)

- 花火大会に行きましょう — "Let's go to the fireworks display"
- 浴衣、似合ってるね — "That yukata suits you"
- 屋台で何か食べようよ — "Let's eat something from the stalls"
- Custom: wearing a yukata and geta sandals for the evening
- Custom: playing festival games like goldfish scooping (金魚すくい) and shooting galleries
- Custom: watching fireworks from a sheet or riverbank, sometimes calling out たまや〜！ (a traditional fireworks-watching shout)
- Custom: joining the circle for Bon dance (盆踊り) around a central yagura tower

#### Sentences & Phrases

- 浴衣を着て祭りに行きます — I'm wearing a yukata to the festival.

- 太鼓の音が聞こえます — I can hear the sound of the taiko drums.
- かき氷を食べたいです — I want to eat shaved ice.
- 人がすごく多いですね — There are so many people, aren't there.
- 提灯がきれいに並んでいます — The lanterns are lined up beautifully.
- 最後の花火は特に大きかったです — The last firework was especially big.

#### What it is & what to say/do
お盆は毎年八月中旬（地域によっては七月）に行われる、先祖の霊を迎えて供養する行事です。多くの人が実家に帰省し、家族でお墓参りをします。会社もお盆休みになることが多いです。
(Obon is an event held in mid-August, or July in some regions, each year to welcome and honor the spirits of ancestors. Many people return to their hometowns and visit the family grave together. Companies often give an Obon holiday too.)

- お盆に帰省しますか — "Are you going back home for Obon?"
- お墓参りに行ってきます — "I'm off to visit the family grave" (said before leaving the house)
- ご先祖様に手を合わせる — to press one's hands together in prayer for one's ancestors
- Custom: lighting a welcoming fire (迎え火) to guide ancestors' spirits home, and a send-off fire (送り火) to see them off again
- Custom: visiting and cleaning the family grave, offering flowers, incense, and food (お供え物)
- Custom: many businesses close for about a week (盆休み), causing heavy travel congestion (帰省ラッシュ)
- Custom: some regions hold Bon Odori dances during this same period

#### Sentences & Phrases
- 今年のお盆はいつ帰省しますか — When are you going back home for Obon this year?
- お盆休みは何日ありますか — How many days is the Obon holiday?
- 家族でお墓参りに行きました — I went to visit the family grave with my family.
- 新幹線がすごく混んでいました — The bullet train was really crowded.
- 帰省ラッシュで渋滞がひどいです — There's terrible traffic from the Obon travel rush.
- お墓を掃除して、花をお供えしました — I cleaned the grave and offered flowers.
- 迎え火を焚いて、ご先祖様を迎えます — We light a welcoming fire to greet our ancestors.
- 実家でゆっくり過ごしました — I spent a relaxing time at my parents' house.
- 親戚みんなに久しぶりに会いました — I saw all my relatives for the first time in a while.
- 線香をあげてきました — I offered incense (at the grave/altar).
- お盆が終わったら、また東京に戻ります — Once Obon is over, I'll go back to Tokyo again.

#### Conversation
A: お盆はどこかに帰省するの？ — Are you going back to your hometown for Obon?
B: うん、実家に帰るよ。お墓参りもしないと — Yeah, I'm going back to my parents' house. I need to visit the family grave too.
A: 新幹線のチケットはもう取った？ — Have you already gotten your bullet train ticket?
B: まだなんだけど、この時期は帰省ラッシュで大変そう — Not yet, but this time of year seems tough because of the Obon travel rush.
A: 早めに予約した方がいいよ — You should book early.
B: そうだね。お盆休みは何日あるの？ — Right. How many days is your Obon holiday?
A: 五日間あるよ。ゆっくり実家で過ごすつもり — I have five days. I'm planning to relax at my parents' place.
B: いいね。ご先祖様によろしく伝えてね — Nice. Say hi to your ancestors for me.

#### What it is & what to say/do
秋は紅葉狩りといって、山や公園で色づいた紅葉を見に行く季節です。栗や柿、さつまいもなど秋の味覚も楽しみます。日本では「食欲の秋」「読書の秋」のように、秋は何かをするのにいい季節だとよく言われます。
(Autumn is the season for "momiji-gari," going to the mountains or parks to view the colored autumn leaves. People also enjoy autumn flavors like chestnuts, persimmons, and sweet potatoes. In Japan, autumn is often described as a good season for doing things, as in the phrases "the autumn of appetite" and "the autumn of reading.")

- 紅葉が綺麗ですね — "The autumn leaves are beautiful, aren't they"
- 紅葉狩りに行きましょう — "Let's go leaf-viewing"
- 涼しくなりましたね — a common seasonal small-talk opener, "It's gotten cooler, hasn't it"
- 食欲の秋ですね — said while eating well in the fall, "It's the season for a big appetite"
- Custom: hiking or driving to famous foliage spots and photographing the colors
- Custom: seasonal food talk — eating sanma, chestnuts, sweet potatoes, and persimmons as they come into season
- Custom: commenting on the shortening days and cooler mornings/evenings as small talk

#### Sentences & Phrases
- 紅葉はいつ頃見頃になりますか — Around when will the autumn leaves be at their best?
- 紅葉前線が南下しています — The autumn-leaf front is moving south.
- 山の紅葉を見に行きたいです — I want to go see the autumn leaves in the mountains.
- 栗ご飯を作りました — I made chestnut rice.
- さつまいもが美味しい季節ですね — It's the season when sweet potatoes are delicious.
- 朝晩は涼しくなってきましたね — It's gotten cool in the mornings and evenings, hasn't it.
- 食欲の秋で、つい食べ過ぎてしまいます — It's the "season of appetite," so I end up eating too much.
- 落ち葉がたくさん道に落ちています — There are lots of fallen leaves on the road.
- 秋刀魚を焼いて食べました — I grilled and ate some saury.
- 日が短くなってきましたね — The days have gotten shorter, haven't they.
- 紅葉狩りにおすすめの場所はありますか — Do you have any recommended spots for leaf-viewing?

#### Conversation
A: だいぶ涼しくなってきましたね — It's gotten quite a bit cooler, hasn't it.
B: そうですね、秋らしくなりました — Yeah, it's really starting to feel like autumn.
A: 週末、紅葉狩りに行きませんか — Would you like to go leaf-viewing this weekend?
B: いいですね。どこか、おすすめの場所はありますか — Sounds good. Do you have any recommended spots?

B: 楽しみです。お昼は栗ご飯とか食べたいですね — I'm looking forward to it. I'd like to eat something like chestnut rice for lunch.
A: いいですね、食欲の秋ですし — Sounds great, it is the season for a big appetite after all.
B: 写真もたくさん撮りましょう — Let's take lots of photos too.

| 明ける | あける | to dawn, to begin (of a year) | N3 |

#### What it is & what to say/do
お正月は一年の始まりを祝う行事で、年が明けると「あけましておめでとうございます」と挨拶します。初詣で神社にお参りし、一年の願い事をします。家族でおせち料理を食べ、お年玉を子供にあげる習慣もあります。
(New Year's celebrates the start of the year; when the new year begins, people greet each other with "Happy New Year." People visit a shrine for the first time in the new year (hatsumōde) and make a wish for the year ahead. Families eat traditional New Year's food (osechi), and it's customary to give children New Year's money (otoshidama).)

- あけましておめでとうございます — "Happy New Year," the standard greeting used after the new year begins
- 今年もよろしくお願いします — "Please treat me kindly again this year," usually said right alongside the New Year's greeting
- 初詣に行きましょう — "Let's go to the first shrine visit"
- 良いお年を — "Have a good year(-end)," a parting greeting said before December 31st
- Custom: at the shrine for hatsumōde, throwing a coin, bowing twice, clapping twice, praying, then bowing once more (二礼二拍手一礼)
- Custom: drawing an おみくじ fortune slip and tying it to a rack if the luck is bad
- Custom: eating osechi (boxed New Year's dishes) and toshikoshi soba on New Year's Eve
- Custom: giving otoshidama (money in a small decorated envelope) to children
- Custom: listening for the 除夜の鐘 (108 temple bell rings at midnight)

#### Sentences & Phrases
- あけましておめでとうございます。今年もよろしくお願いします — Happy New Year. Please treat me well again this year.
- 初詣に行きましたか — Did you go to the first shrine visit of the year?
- おせち料理はもう食べましたか — Have you already eaten osechi?
- 今年はどんな願い事をしましたか — What did you wish for this year?
- おみくじを引きました。大吉でした — I drew a fortune slip. It was great luck.
- 年越しそばを食べながら紅白を見ました — I watched Kōhaku while eating New Year's Eve soba.
- お年玉をたくさんもらいました — I received a lot of New Year's money.
- 除夜の鐘を聞きに行きました — I went to listen to the New Year's Eve temple bells.
- 良いお年をお迎えください — I hope you have a good New Year (said before the year ends).
- 初日の出を見に行く予定です — I'm planning to go see the first sunrise of the year.
- 神社は人ですごく混んでいました — The shrine was extremely crowded with people.

#### Conversation
A: あけましておめでとうございます — Happy New Year.
B: あけましておめでとうございます。今年もよろしくお願いします — Happy New Year. Please treat me well again this year too.
A: もう初詣に行きましたか — Have you already been to the first shrine visit?
B: はい、元旦に行きました。おみくじも引きましたよ — Yes, I went on New Year's Day. I drew a fortune slip too.
A: どうでしたか — How was it?
B: 中吉でした。まあまあですね — It was medium luck. Not bad, not great.

#### What it is & what to say/do

(In Japan, Christmas is popular less as a family event and more as a romantic event for couples to spend together. Valentine's Day is a day when women give chocolate to men — "true feelings" chocolate to someone they like, and "obligation" chocolate to coworkers. A month later, on White Day, men give a return gift.)

- メリークリスマス — "Merry Christmas"
- クリスマスデートしよう — "Let's go on a Christmas date"
- チョコレート、どうぞ — "Here, please have some chocolate" (giving Valentine's chocolate)
- 義理チョコです — "This is obligation chocolate" (clarifying it isn't romantic)
- お返し、ありがとう — "Thank you for the return gift" (said on White Day)
- Custom: couples booking a fancy dinner/date and viewing illumination light displays for Christmas
- Custom: buying a decorated Christmas cake, even though the "couples' day" framing is separate from the family cake tradition
- Custom: women giving chocolate to men on Valentine's Day (reversed from Western custom) — 本命チョコ to someone they like vs. 義理チョコ to coworkers/friends out of politeness
- Custom: men reciprocating with a gift (often cookies, candy, or something white) on White Day, one month later

- バレンタインデーにチョコを渡しました — I gave chocolate on Valentine's Day.
- これは義理チョコだから気にしないでね — This is obligation chocolate, so don't read too much into it.
- 本命チョコは誰にあげるの — Who are you giving your "true feelings" chocolate to?
- ホワイトデーのお返しは何がいいと思いますか — What do you think would be a good return gift for White Day?

- 夜景がとても綺麗でした — The night view was very beautiful.
- 二人で静かに過ごしたいです — I want to spend a quiet time together, just the two of us.

A: いいね。クリスマスケーキは買うの？ — Nice. Are you going to buy a Christmas cake?
B: うん、もう予約してあるよ — Yeah, I've already reserved one.
A: バレンタインデーは？何かあげるつもり？ — What about Valentine's Day? Are you planning to give her anything?
B: それは逆で、僕がもらう側だよ。日本では女性が渡すからね — That's the reverse — I'm the one receiving. In Japan, it's the women who give.
A: そうだった！じゃあホワイトデーにお返しするんだね — That's right! Then you'll give a return gift on White Day.
B: そう、来月忘れないようにしないと — Right, I need to make sure I don't forget next month.

#### What it is & what to say/do
日本では三月に卒業式、四月に入学式や入社式が行われ、新しい年度が始まります。多くの企業もこの四月始まりの年度に合わせて人事異動を行います。大学生は卒業前年から就職活動、いわゆる就活を始めます。
(In Japan, graduation ceremonies are held in March, and entrance/company-entry ceremonies in April, marking the start of a new academic and fiscal year. Many companies also carry out personnel transfers to align with this April-start year. University students begin job hunting, called "shūkatsu," the year before graduation.)

- ご卒業おめでとうございます — "Congratulations on your graduation"
- ご入学おめでとうございます — "Congratulations on starting school"
- 就活、頑張ってね — "Good luck with your job hunting"
- 内定もらったよ — "I got a job offer"
- 異動になりました — "I've been transferred (to a new department/location)"
- Custom: students wearing formal suits or hakama to graduation ceremonies, exchanging yearbooks, and the "second button" tradition (第二ボタン) of giving one's uniform button to a person one likes upon graduation
- Custom: new employees (新入社員) attending a company entrance ceremony in early April, often followed by training
- Custom: job-hunting students wearing a near-identical black recruit suit (リクルートスーツ) to interviews and company information sessions
- Custom: April 1st personnel transfers/reassignments (人事異動) taking effect company-wide

- 来週、面接があります — I have an interview next week.
- 四月から新しい部署に異動します — Starting in April, I'll be transferred to a new department.
- 新年度が始まると忙しくなります — Once the new fiscal year starts, things get busy.
- 入学式で新しい友達ができました — I made new friends at the entrance ceremony.
- リクルートスーツを買いました — I bought a recruit suit (for job hunting).

#### Conversation
A: 就活はどう？順調？ — How's job hunting going? Smoothly?
B: 実は、先週内定をもらったんだ — Actually, I got a job offer last week.
A: おめでとう！どこの会社？ — Congratulations! Which company?
B: IT系の会社だよ。四月から新入社員として働くんだ — It's an IT company. I'll be working there as a new employee starting in April.
