# 11. Everyday Life at Home / Family (家での日常会話)

*Companion deep-dive to ../conversation-topics.md — expands topic #11 into kanji, vocabulary,
sentences, and practice conversations for each subtopic.*

### Register note

Home life is talked about in **plain/casual form**, not polite です/ます. Verbs conjugate to
their dictionary/plain-negative/plain-past forms, invitations use ~しよう ("let's..."), and
requests use casual imperatives or ~て (て-form) rather than ~てください. Polite form only
resurfaces when addressing someone outside the family (e.g. a nursery teacher — see subtopic 10).

| Polite (です/ます) | Plain (casual) | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 食べます | 食べる | eat |
| 食べません | 食べない | don't eat |
| 片付けます | 片付ける | tidy up |
| 行きましょう | 行こう | let's go |
| 作りましょう | 作ろう | let's make |
| 見てください | 見て | look / watch (casual request) |

- 起きて！朝だよ。 — Wake up! It's morning.
- よく眠れた？ — Did you sleep well?

- まだ眠い… — I'm still sleepy...
- 早く起きて、幼稚園に遅れるよ。 — Get up quickly, you'll be late for kindergarten.
- 顔を洗って、歯を磨いてね。 — Wash your face and brush your teeth.

- もう起きる時間だよ。 — It's already time to get up.

#### Conversation
A: 起きて〜、朝だよ！ — Wake up, it's morning!
B: うーん、まだ眠い… — Ugh, I'm still sleepy...
A: よく眠れた？ — Did you sleep well?

A: じゃあ、早く起きて。顔を洗ってね。 — Then get up quickly. Go wash your face.
B: はーい。今日は何を着る？ — Okay. What should I wear today?
A: この青いシャツはどう？ — How about this blue shirt?
B: うん、それでいい。 — Yeah, that's fine.
A: じゃあ、着替えたら朝ごはんを作ろう。 — Then once you're changed, let's make breakfast.
B: 何が食べたい？ — What do you want to eat?
A: パンと卵がいいな。 — I feel like bread and eggs.
B: いいね、それにしよう。 — Sounds good, let's do that.
