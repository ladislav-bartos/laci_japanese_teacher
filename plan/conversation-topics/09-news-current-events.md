#### Vocabulary

| 信じる | しんじる | to believe | N3 |

#### Sentences & Phrases
- 〜のニュース見た? — Did you see the news about ~?
- あのニュース聞いた? — Did you hear that news?
- 本当に?驚いた。 — Really? I was surprised.
- まさかそんなことが起こるとは思わなかった。 — I never thought something like that would happen.
- それは心配ですね。 — That's worrying, isn't it.
- 信じられないニュースだね。 — That's unbelievable news.
- それ、気になるニュースだね。 — That's a news story that caught my attention.
- 大変なことになったね。 — That turned into a big deal, didn't it.
- どこでそのニュースを知ったの? — Where did you learn about that news?
- 最近、いろいろな事件がありますね。 — There have been a lot of incidents lately.
- なんだかショックなニュースだったね。 — That was kind of a shocking piece of news, wasn't it.

#### Conversation
A: ねえ、昨日のニュース見た? — Hey, did you see yesterday's news?
B: ううん、見てない。何かあったの? — No, I didn't see it. Did something happen?
A: 近くの駅前で火事があったんだって。 — Apparently there was a fire near the station.
B: え、本当に?それは心配だね。けが人はいたの? — Really? That's worrying. Was anyone hurt?
A: ううん、幸い誰もけがしなかったみたい。 — No, luckily it seems no one was hurt.
B: よかった。でも、びっくりしたでしょう。 — That's good. But you must have been surprised.
A: うん、まさかあんなに近くで火事が起こるとは思わなかった。 — Yeah, I never thought a fire would happen that close.
B: 最近、いろいろな事件やニュースが多いね。 — There have been a lot of incidents and news stories lately.
A: 本当に。ちょっと心配になるよね。 — True. It does make you a bit worried.

#### Sentences & Phrases
- 最近、物価が上がってるよね。 — Prices have been going up lately, haven't they.
- 円安のせいで、輸入品が高くなった。 — Because of the weak yen, imported goods have gotten expensive.
- 給料はあまり変わらないのに、生活費だけ上がってる。 — My salary hasn't changed much, but only the cost of living keeps rising.
- 最近、節約を心がけてるんだ。 — I've been trying to save money lately.
- ガソリン代も上がったよね。 — Gas prices have gone up too, haven't they.
- 円のレート、最近どう? — How's the yen exchange rate lately?
- 前より外食が高く感じる。 — Eating out feels more expensive than before.
- 税金も上がって大変だよね。 — Taxes have gone up too, it's tough, isn't it.
- インフレの影響で色々値上がりしてるね。 — Because of inflation, lots of things are getting more expensive.
- うちも家計が厳しくなってきた。 — Our household finances have gotten tighter too.
- 円安は旅行者にはいいけど、生活は大変だよね。 — A weak yen is good for tourists, but daily life is tough.

#### Conversation
A: 最近、物価がどんどん上がってて大変だね。 — Prices keep going up lately, it's rough.
B: 本当に。スーパーの食品もずいぶん値上がりしたよね。 — Really. Groceries at the supermarket have gotten a lot more expensive too.
A: 円安の影響もあると思う。 — I think the weak yen has an effect too.
B: そうだね。輸入品は特に高くなったよね。 — Yeah. Imported goods especially have gotten expensive.
A: 給料はあまり上がらないのに、税金や生活費だけ増えてる気がする。 — My salary hasn't gone up much, but it feels like only taxes and living costs are increasing.
B: わかる。うちも最近、節約するようにしてるよ。 — I get it. My family has been trying to save money lately too.
A: 何か節約のコツある? — Do you have any money-saving tips?
B: 外食を減らして、家で料理するようにしてるかな。 — I've been cutting back on eating out and cooking at home more.
