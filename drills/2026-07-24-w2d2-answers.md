## 文法 — Choice — 2/5 (+1 half-correct)

| # | Question | Your answer | Correct | ✅/❌ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | やり（かけた／ぬいた）／やり（かけよう／ぬこう） | a, a | a, b | half ❌ |
| 2 | 信じ（きって／かけて）いた人にだまされた | b | a | ❌ |
| 3 | あり（えない／うない） | a | a | ✅ |
| 4 | 起こり（える／うる）だろう | b | a or b (both OK) | ✅ |
| 5 | わかり（きった／かけた）ことだ | b | a | ❌ |

**Notes on the misses:**
- **#1** — first blank correct (やり**かけた**ことは = "things you've started"), but second blank
  should be **ぬこう** (やり抜こう = "let's carry them through to the end"), not かけよう. かける
  means "to begin/be partway through" — you can't "begin" something "to the end," so やりかけよう
  doesn't fit the meaning of finishing what was started.
- **#2** — correct is **きって** (信じきっていた = "trusted completely/with all my heart"), not
  かけて. 〜切る conveys doing something fully/completely, which matches 心から (from the heart);
  〜かける would mean "started to believe," a much weaker and less natural fit before "だまされた"
  (was deceived).
- **#4** — good catch: both える and うる are grammatically valid here (unlike the negative form,
  where only ありえない works, never ありうない) — this was one of today's teaching points, and you
  applied it correctly.
- **#5** — correct is **きった** (わかりきったことだ = "something already completely obvious/
  understood"), not かけた. This is the same 〜切る＝complete pattern as #2 — わかりかけた would mean
  "started to understand," which doesn't fit "そんなことはあなたに言われるまでもなく" (something so
  obvious it didn't need saying).

**Pattern worth noting:** three of today's four misses (#1's second blank, #2, #5) all involve
choosing かける over きる/ぬく when the sentence calls for *completion*, not a *partial/starting*
action. かける (Pattern 1) and 切る／抜く (Patterns 2/4) are opposite ends of the same spectrum —
worth a focused side-by-side drill on "just started" vs. "all the way through" before Day 3.
