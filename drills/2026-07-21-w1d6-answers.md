  visiting for the first time) directly maps to 初診, not 再診.
- **外科 (#8)**, not 内科 — 外科 (surgery) is the department for procedures that cut into the
  body; 内科 (internal medicine) treats illness without surgery. "お腹を切る手術" (an operation

**Notes on the misses:**
- **預ける (#1)**, not 振り込んで — 預ける is "to deposit/entrust" money to the bank for keeping
  (お金を銀行に預ける＝put money into your own savings). 振り込む is "to transfer" money *to* a

  out every single night" as something excessive. 手ごろ means "reasonably priced/affordable" —
  the opposite framing, and doesn't fit a sentence implying disapproval of a habit.
- **引き落とされて (#5)**, not 引き出されて — 引き落とす is specifically "to auto-debit" a bill
  (公共料金が自動で引き落とされる), matching 自動で in the sentence. 引き出す is "to withdraw

  doesn't fit — you don't "economize nutrition," and the sentence structure (栄養が〜ないように)
  needs an intransitive verb describing a state, which かたよる is and 節約する isn't.

## 文法 — Choice — 1/5 (+1 invalid, +1 unanswered)

| 2 | 人の悪口を言っ（a.たら/b.ては）ならない。 | a | b | ❌ |
| 3 | もうこれ以上（a.待ったら/b.待っては）いられない。 | b | b | ✅ |
| 4 | 忙しくて休んで（a.ばかりは/b.だけは）いられない。 | 2 (invalid — not a/b) | a | — |
| 5 | そんなにゆっくりし（a.てらんない/b.てはならない）。 | (blank) | a | — |

**Notes on the misses:**
- **ねば (#1)**, not れば — 守らねばならない is the fixed pattern V-ない(stem)+ねばならない
  ("must do X"). れば is a plain conditional and doesn't combine with ならない this way — there's
  no grammatical "〜ればならない" pattern.
- **ては (#2)**, not たら — 言ってはならない is the prohibition pattern V-て+はならない ("must not
  do X"). たらならない isn't a valid construction; たら is a conditional connector, not part of
  this pattern.
- **#4 (invalid)** — your answer "2" doesn't match either option (a/b), so this reads as an
  accidental format slip (same issue flagged on Day 5) rather than a real answer. For reference,
  correct is **a**: 休んでばかりはいられない — ばかりは is the fixed particle combination for this
  pattern (〜てばかりは／も いられない); だけは changes the meaning and isn't the grammar point
  being tested.
- **#5 (unanswered)** — correct is **a**: てらんない is the casual contraction of てはいられない /
  てられない (can't afford the time/leisure to do something), which fits "そんなにゆっくり" ("that
  leisurely"). てはならない is the prohibition pattern (#2's grammar point) and doesn't fit this
  context — nothing here is being prohibited, it's about lacking spare time.

**Recommendation:** all four grammar patterns from today (〜ねばならない, 〜てはならない,
〜て(は)いられない, 〜てばかりはいられない) look superficially similar (formal "must/can't"
constructions) but attach differently and mean different things — this is the section to prioritize
review on, since only 1 of 5 was answered correctly and confidently.
