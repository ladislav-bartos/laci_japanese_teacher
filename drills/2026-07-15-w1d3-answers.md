| 4 | エレベーターは（a.点検中/b.検問中）のため、お待ちください。 | b | a | ❌ |

**Notes:**
- **立入厳禁 = 入ってはいけない** ("strictly forbidden to enter"). 厳禁 means "strictly prohibited," so it's always the "must NOT" reading, never "must."
  - 「危険ですので、立入厳禁です。」("It's dangerous, so entry is strictly forbidden.")
- **点検中 vs 検問中**: 点検 = "inspection" (of equipment/machines, e.g. an elevator). 検問 = "checkpoint/inspection" in the sense of police/security stopping people or vehicles — wrong register for an elevator.
  - 「エレベーターは点検中です。」("The elevator is under inspection.") vs 「この先、検問中です。」("There's a checkpoint ahead.")

**Notes on the misses:**
- **値段はともかく** (#2) — 〜はともかく = "setting X aside / regardless of X," used when X's status doesn't matter to the point being made. まだしも means "X would be tolerable, but not Y" — a comparison of degree, which doesn't fit "値段" here (there's no "price would be tolerable" framing, just "regardless of price, the taste is #1").
  - 「値段はともかく、味はこの店が一番だ。」("Price aside, this shop's flavor is #1.")
- **抜きでは** (#4) — the taught form for "without X, [negative outcome]" needs は attached before a negative-potential clause: 抜きでは／抜きにしては／抜きには + Vれない. Bare 抜きにして (without は) doesn't fit directly before 成功しなかっただろう.
  - 「彼抜きでは、このプロジェクトは成功しなかっただろう。」

**Notes:**
- **席を外しております = "(they are) currently away from their desk/not in"** — a standard polite way to say someone isn't at their desk right now, not that they're upset.
  - 「部長は只今、席を外しております。後ほどお電話いたします。」
- **お召し上がりください = "please eat/drink"** (お召し上がり is the honorific form of 食べる/飲む). 着る ("wear") uses a different honorific (お召しになる) that looks similar in kanji (召) but is a different verb entirely — that's likely what caused the mix-up.
  - 「どうぞお召し上がりください。」("Please, go ahead and eat.") vs 「素敵な服をお召しになっていますね。」("You're wearing lovely clothes.")
- **申し訳ございません = "I'm very sorry"** — this is an apology phrase (謙譲語 for すみません), so the speaker (部下) is apologizing/showing a contrite attitude, not being angry.
  - 「申し訳ございません、すぐに確認いたします。」("I'm very sorry, I'll check right away.")
